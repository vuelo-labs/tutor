# Assessment: Mode bleed — machine mode leaking into human relationships

**Proposed addition:** The site argues you should stop spending human energy on machines. The refined idea is the reverse: what if machine mode bleeds into human contexts? Not screen time, not addiction — specifically: you stay in task-focused, transactional, emotionally efficient mode when you switch from a machine conversation to a human one. The message would be: a real mode switch matters, check which mode you are actually in before you respond to someone.

**Assessed by:** Editorial review against existing site content, voice, audience, and argument structure.

**Prior assessment:** The previous "AI addiction / screen time" idea was rejected because the site already handles it structurally, and because the framing was generic. This is a different idea. Assessing it fresh.

---

## 1. Is this already said?

**It is gestured at, but never named.**

The manifesto comes close in one paragraph:

> "And by the time you get home to the people you actually love — the people who you want to give your warmth and your attention and your presence to — you can be depleted, running on fumes or unable to come down from a day of SO MUCH PROGRESS."

That sentence describes the *symptom* (depletion, being wired) but frames it as an energy problem — you spent your battery on the machine, so you arrive home empty. The cause is waste. You gave warmth to something that could not receive it, and now you have none left.

The mode bleed idea is a different mechanism. It is not that you are empty. It is that you are still in the wrong mode. You might have plenty of energy, but you are running it through machine-mode logic: transactional, task-oriented, efficient. You respond to your partner the way you would respond to a prompt — get to the point, solve the problem, move on. The issue is not depletion. It is contamination.

The manifesto never names this. The guide does not address it. The course does not address it. The homepage mode cards define what each mode *is* but do not warn about one leaking into the other.

**Verdict on question 1: This idea is genuinely absent from the site.**

---

## 2. Thematic fit

**It strengthens the argument. It does not muddy it.**

The site's thesis is directional: stop spending human energy on machines. The mode bleed idea completes the picture by pointing in the other direction: stop spending machine energy on humans. That is not a contradiction. It is the same principle — know which mode you are in — applied symmetrically.

The site already insists that modes are completely different. "Two modes. Completely different. Know which one you're in. That's the whole skill." If the whole skill is knowing which mode you are in, then a failure to switch modes when the context changes is the most natural failure case of that skill. The site describes one version of the failure (human mode applied to machines). This would describe the other (machine mode applied to humans).

Crucially, the site does not need a new concept for this. The vocabulary already exists: human mode, machine mode, switching, noticing. Mode bleed is not a new framework bolted on. It is a consequence of the framework the site already teaches.

The risk of muddying is low because the site's argument has always been about direction, not just conservation. It is not purely "save your energy." It is "each mode belongs in its context." The reverse direction completes that, it does not complicate it.

---

## 3. Voice fit

**It can be said in the site's voice — but only if it stays observational.**

The danger is real. "Are you being transactional with the people you love?" sounds like a therapy prompt. "Check in with yourself before you respond" sounds like a mindfulness app. The site cannot say either of those things without breaking character.

But the site's voice works by describing what is happening and letting the reader recognise it. That is the test: can this idea be described as an observation rather than an instruction?

Yes. Consider: "You've been in machine mode all day. Task, output, next. Task, output, next. Then someone you love asks how your day was, and you give them a status update." That is observational. It does not tell you what to do. It holds up the mirror and lets you see which mode you are in. That is how the manifesto works.

The voice will hold as long as the copy stays in the territory of "here is what might be happening" and never crosses into "here is what you should do about it in your relationship." The moment it offers relational advice, it becomes self-help. The site's job is to name the pattern. The reader's job is to act on it.

---

## 4. Audience fit

**This is closer to the audience's reality than the addiction framing was, but it is still a stretch for the primary audience.**

The previous assessment noted that the primary audience is people learning to use AI at work — beginners, not power users. The addiction concern was rejected partly because it addressed a problem the audience does not yet have.

Mode bleed is different. You do not need to be a heavy AI user to experience it. Anyone who spends a full day in task-execution mode — AI or not — can carry that mode home. The AI just makes it more pronounced because machine mode is explicitly transactional in a way that, say, a spreadsheet is not. You are literally practising a communication style all day, and that style is the opposite of what your relationships need.

That said, the site's beginner audience is still learning to send their first message. They are not yet spending full days in machine mode. The people who would recognise mode bleed are the ones who have been using AI regularly enough for it to become a default mental state at work. That is the intermediate-to-advanced user, not the beginner.

This limits where the idea can live. It does not belong in the early course. It does not belong on the homepage. It belongs somewhere that speaks to people who have already built the habit — the manifesto, or a later course moment.

---

## 5. Placement

**The manifesto is the only place where this earns its weight.**

- **Homepage mode cards:** No. The cards define the modes. Adding a warning about mode bleed to a definition would confuse the introduction. The reader needs to understand the modes before they can understand how one leaks into the other.

- **Guide:** No. The guide is practical and task-focused. Mode bleed is a reflective observation, not a technique. It would break the guide's rhythm, which is: here is how to do this better.

- **Course (early modules):** No. Beginners need confidence, not caution about a failure mode they have not encountered.

- **Course (later modules / stage closing):** Possible but weak. The stage closings already carry "the machine will wait" — adding a second reflective theme would dilute that moment.

- **Manifesto, after "Here's what's actually happening":** Yes. This is the section that describes the cost. It currently describes depletion (you arrive home empty). Mode bleed is the adjacent cost (you arrive home in the wrong mode). It belongs in the same section, as a short extension — not a new heading, but a paragraph or two that completes the picture the manifesto is already painting.

Specifically, the manifesto says: "And by the time you get home to the people you actually love... you can be depleted, running on fumes or unable to come down from a day of SO MUCH PROGRESS." That is the energy-depletion version. Immediately after that, a brief paragraph about mode contamination would land naturally: you are not just empty, you might also be in the wrong gear entirely.

---

## 6. Draft copy test

Two attempts in the site's voice, written as if they follow the "depleted, running on fumes" paragraph in the manifesto:

**Draft A:**

And sometimes it's not even that you're empty. Sometimes you've got plenty left. But you're still in machine mode. Someone asks how you're doing and you give them a status update. Your kid tells you a long, winding story and you're waiting for the point. Your partner needs the slow, circular, going-nowhere conversation — the one that IS the relationship — and you're trying to get to the action item. That's not burnout. That's mode bleed. You forgot to switch back.

**Draft B:**

Or worse — you get home and you've still got energy, but it's the wrong kind. You're efficient. You're solving. Someone starts a conversation and you're already structuring the response before they've finished talking. That is machine mode running in a human context. The person in front of you doesn't need your efficiency. They need the long way round. And you've forgotten how to take it.

---

## 7. Assessment of the drafts

Both drafts hold. Neither tips into self-help. Neither offers advice. They describe a pattern and name it. Draft A is slightly stronger because "you forgot to switch back" ties directly to the site's core skill — noticing which mode you are in. Draft B is warmer but slightly more prescriptive with "they need the long way round."

Neither draft requires a new section heading. Both work as a paragraph inserted after the existing "depleted, running on fumes" paragraph, extending the manifesto's description of the cost.

---

## Summary

This is a genuinely absent idea that fits the site's existing framework without introducing new concepts. The vocabulary is already there. The failure mode is a natural consequence of the site's central argument. It can be written in the site's voice without tipping into self-help, as long as it stays observational. It belongs in one specific place: the manifesto, immediately after the depletion paragraph, as a brief extension — not a new section.

The constraint is: it must stay small. A paragraph, maybe two. It is a completing detail, not a new argument. If it grows into its own section or its own heading, it overweights a secondary point and shifts the manifesto's balance.

---

**VERDICT: CONDITIONAL** — only works as one or two observational paragraphs in the manifesto, inserted after the "depleted, running on fumes" paragraph in "Here's what's actually happening." Must stay within the site's existing mode vocabulary, must not offer relational advice, must not become a section. Draft A above is ready to use. Any version that grows beyond a brief extension or appears anywhere other than the manifesto should be rejected.
