What's actually happening

The machines are getting faster.
We're running out of us.

AI agents are replacing the inefficient moments at work — meetings, the corridor conversation, the ten minutes before a call where you caught up with a colleague as a person. On paper, that looks like progress. But those moments were load-bearing. They were where relationships got maintained and people felt seen.

The result: all cognitive capacity consumed at work, nothing left for the people who matter when the day ends. Not because people are working longer — because the texture of work has changed. There's no fallow time. No breathing room. Just input, decision, output, repeat.

Think First teaches you to get so good at working with machines that it becomes a discrete skill — something you can do quickly, clearly, and then put down. When machine communication stops being a constant cognitive tax, you get some of yourself back.

The framework

Seven skills. One framework.
Every age, every stage.

These aren't separate subjects. They're the same map of how clear thinking works, at every level of capability. What changes is the stakes, the complexity, and the medium — not the underlying skill.

Same skill. Different stakes. A child learns to break a sandwich recipe into ordered steps. An executive learns to break a strategy into testable decisions. Both are practising decomposition — skill 2. The framework is identical. The level is not.
01
Clarity

Turning a vague thought into a precise, answerable question. Most thinking fails here, before it starts.

02
Decomposition

Breaking a complex problem into parts that can actually be solved. The first step in directing any agent — human or AI.

03
Relationships

Seeing how the parts connect and influence each other. The hardest skill — where humans skip and machines can't.

04
Outcome Definition

Knowing exactly what "done" looks like before you start. Impossible to evaluate output without it.

05
Gap Analysis

Finding what's missing between where you are and where you need to be. The skill that makes planning realistic.

06
Procedural Thinking

Writing steps that a literal, non-inferring system can follow. Essential for directing AI with precision.

07
Evaluation

Knowing whether the output is actually good — and why. The skill that keeps you in the driver's seat.

A 4-year-old sorting ingredients and a VP structuring an acquisition proposal are on the same skill tree. Different levels — same framework. Think First places you on each skill independently and trains you from exactly where you are.

How it works

Adaptive. Proactive. Personalised
to how you actually think.

Adaptive assessment

Five minutes, conversational, no right or wrong answers. Think First places you independently on each of the 7 skills — so training starts exactly where you are, not where a curriculum assumes you should be.

Dynamically generated exercises

There is no static content library. AI generates every exercise for your level, context, and goals. A gap analysis challenge for a 9-year-old looks nothing like one for a product manager — and both are exactly right for where they are.

Proactive delivery

Think First doesn't wait for you to log in. It reaches out via Slack, WhatsApp, or the web — at the times you're actually cognitively sharp. The first two weeks learn your rhythm. From week three, it trains you when it counts.

Continuous cognitive profiling

The system builds a model of how you think: which skills are strong, which need work, when you're sharpest, and how you progress over time. That profile compounds — and extends to any skill domain you add next.
